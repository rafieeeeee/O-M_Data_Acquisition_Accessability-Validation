# Project Context: O&M Data Acquisition & Validation

This project builds an empirical data pipeline to derive operational limits for offshore wind Operations and Maintenance (O&M) vessels. It replaces static heuristics with dynamic, vessel-aware workability surfaces, while keeping vessel roles evidence-led rather than inferred from length or name heuristics.

Current modelling boundary: Stage 1 exists as an observed/provisional workability surface with an $H_s \times T_p$ default view. Stage 2 is the next planned modelling step and has not started. The completed Fusion v2 evidence-readiness audit recommends `proceed_with_restrictions`: wave-only and wave+wind-speed can be primary sensitivity lanes, current-aware comparisons are NWS-domain and coverage-limited sensitivity only, wind direction is excluded from primary predictors, depth-warning exclusion/sensitivity is required, and no calibrated `P(operation | weather)` access-probability claim is supported.

## Domain Concepts

### AIS (Automatic Identification System)
- **Source:** Danish Maritime Authority (DMA) S3 archives.
- **Data Structure:** Longitudinal study currently implemented for broad 2010-2025 farm-candidate slices, with the Wikinger/Baltic 2010-2020 pilot preserved as a historical validation cluster. The runner prioritizes quarterly coverage (`Jan/Apr/Jul/Oct`) before filling remaining months.
- **Filtering:** 
    - **Regional:** European Master Box (46.5N–60.0N, -4.5E–15.0E).
    - **Proximity & Tiers:** Precision asset-proximity checks use a `scipy.spatial.KDTree` to detect vessel dwells across a 4-tier taxonomy:
        - **Tier A (Asset-Proximal):** $\le 200\text{ m}$ to nearest turbine point, SOG $\le 1.0\text{ kn}$, duration $\ge 20\text{ min}$.
        - **Tier B (Farm-Internal):** Completely inside the wind farm polygon boundary, SOG $\le 1.0\text{ kn}$, duration $\ge 30\text{ min}$.
        - **Tier C (Slow Operational):** Inside the farm or $\le 500\text{ m}$ asset buffer, $1.0\text{ kn} < \text{SOG} \le 3.0\text{ kn}$, duration $\ge 20\text{ min}$.
        - **Tier D (Context Holding):** Inside the outer context box, outside the farm polygon, SOG $\le 1.0\text{ kn}$, duration $\ge 30\text{ min}$.

### The "10-Minute Backbone"
- A rigid temporal grid where AIS, metocean (NORA3), and structural sensor (RAVE) data are synchronized.
- **Target Status:** Labeling events as "Success" (Active Maintenance) or "Wait-on-Weather" (WoW) using SCADA/DPR ground truth.

### Offshore Wind Infrastructure
- **Open European Offshore Wind Turbine Database:** The primary source for turbine coordinates and farm boundaries.
- **Wikinger Wind Farm:** The primary focus area and pilot site for validation, selected for its high volume of Service Operation Vessel (SOV) activity.
- **German Bight Mega-Arrays:** The spatial domain has been expanded to include Alpha Ventus and Trianel Windpark Borkum I & II using Mode B extraction.
- **European Dwell Harvest:** The dwell backfill runner can now resolve `european_master` and `germany` dynamically from `Data/Interim/European_Turbine_Coordinates.csv`. Dynamic clusters process farms with at least three coordinate points, preserving the Wikinger/Baltic pilot while allowing broad empirical dwell-event collection across the European coordinate universe.
- **Offshore Substations (OSS):** The spatial logic explicitly preserves an `is_substation` boolean flag during KDTree matching. This infrastructure node flagging prevents logistics campaigns at substation hubs from polluting standard turbine workability curves.

### Fleet & Vessel Vocabulary
- **Vessel Classification & Taxonomy:** The system no longer derives operational vessel classes from vessel length, vessel name, or keyword heuristics. Authoritative vessel taxonomy is now sourced directly from the raw AIS / DMA fleet registry ship type field. Continuous vessel length is retained separately as `vessel_length_m` and is used strictly for exploratory filtering, not for programmatic classification.
- **Legacy Compatibility:** The legacy `vessel_class` column remains solely for backward compatibility with existing scripts, reports, and unit tests. It acts as a direct alias of the raw ship type label/value and must not be interpreted as a derived operational class. CTV, SOV, HLV, jack-up, or crane role categories are not derived or inferred dynamically unless provided by a validated external registry. Missing physical dimension data is backfilled from a local DuckDB offline fleet registry.

### RQ9 Maintenance Intervention Intensity
- **Interpretation Boundary:** RQ9 uses AIS dwell behaviour as maintenance intervention-intensity evidence, not confirmed failure-rate evidence. A vessel visit is not automatically a failure; true failure inference requires SCADA, fault-log, work-order, or equivalent validation.
- **Evidence Levels:** The RQ9 evidence ladder separates observation coverage, vessel presence, candidate intervention, repeated/long intervention, SCADA-linked intervention, and confirmed failure. Levels based only on AIS support intervention intensity, not validated faults.
- **Farm and Turbine Outputs:** Farm-level phase-separated intervention intensity, turbine feasibility, turbine denominator/exposure v1, and turbine characteristics comparisons now exist under `Data/Processed/analysis/rq9_intervention_intensity/` and `reports/rq9_intervention_intensity/`.
- **Sea-Basin Caveat:** `sea_basin` is a geographic grouping label, not a physical exposure metric. The Baltic/North Sea contrast is strong in the AIS-derived intervention-intensity proxy, but it remains entangled with country/farm composition, vessel concentration, assignment geometry, CTV/SOV detectability, and AIS observability bias.
- **AIS Observability Bias:** Direct AIS receiver/source geometry is not available in the current RQ9 tables. Receiver station, terrestrial/satellite flag, receiver coordinates, distance-to-nearest receiver, or accepted offshore-distance proxies are required before treating sea-basin differences as operational or reliability differences.

## Data Pipeline Architecture

### Funnel Approach (Hybrid)
The pipeline operates in two modes to balance storage efficiency with auditability:
1. **Mode A (Regional Slice):** Streams and filters large monthly archives into regional CSVs (e.g., German Bight). Retains all traffic below a speed threshold. Used for validation months.
2. **Mode B (Farm-Candidate Extraction):** Extracts only AIS pings within a configurable buffer (e.g., 2nm) of known wind farm bounding boxes. Used for standard longitudinal slices to minimize disk footprint.

### Processing Tiers
1. **Tier 1 (Raw):** Ingested AIS CSVs (from Mode A or Mode B). Stored in `Data/Raw/`.
2. **Tier 2 (Interim):** Identified candidate O&M vessels and consolidated dwell events. Stored in `Data/Interim/`.
3. **Tier 3 (Processed):** Synchronized AIS, Metocean, and SCADA features in the "10-Minute Backbone". Stored in `Data/Processed/`.
4. **Catalog Layer:** A local **DuckDB** database (`Data/catalog.duckdb`) provides a SQL interface for cross-slice analysis and feature engineering.

## Metocean & Synchronization
- **FINO1:** 10-minute ground-truth wave spectra ($H_s, T_p, \theta$).
- **NORA3 Wave Ingestion:** Hourly 3km wave hindcast data is pulled from MET Norway's THREDDS server. The backbone extracts wave parameters: significant wave height (`hs`), peak period (`tp`), and wave direction (`wave_direction`). The wave ingestion utilizes spatial coordinate rounding to 2 decimal places and month-level caching to share raw files across close-proximity turbines.
- **Atmospheric Wind Ingestion:** Wind speed and direction at both 10m and 100m hub heights are extracted from MET Norway's NORA3 Atmospheric hindcast (`nora3_subset_atmos/wind_hourly_v2`). It retrieves variables (`wind_speed`, `wind_direction`) at height dimensions `10` and `100` and saves them locally with coordinate caching.
- **NORA3 Cache Sidecar Consolidation:** While long-running NORA3 downloads are active, completed raw wave/wind cache pairs in `Data/Raw/Metocean/NORA3` can be joined by `scripts/consolidate_nora3_cache.py`. The sidecar only reads stable cache files, writes checkpoint parquet batches to `Data/Processed/metocean/nora3_joined_cache/`, and tracks processed pairs in a manifest so repeated runs do not interrupt the downloader.
- **CMEMS Current Ingestion:** Accepted current evidence must use true Eulerian horizontal components (`uo`, `vo`) from source-labelled Copernicus Marine products, with speed and flow-to direction derived locally. Legacy CMEMS CSV and offline synthetic current paths are not acceptable research evidence and must not be promoted into confidence layers or Fusion tables.
- **Common Regional Metocean Archive Method:** NWS and Baltic regional products must share the wind-farm-based continuous archive method documented in `analysis/06_rq6_metocean_spatial_resolution/COMMON_METOCEAN_EXTRACTION_METHODOLOGY.md` and implemented by `src/om_pipeline/metocean/common_requirements.py`. Product adapters may differ, but requirement identification must use the canonical `common_metocean_farm_requirements` output: farm/turbine metadata, operation or commissioning start, the configured study end date, and footprint-plus-buffer spatial bounds. AIS events are validation samples and supporting diagnostics only; they must not define the continuous archive boundary.
- **Copernicus NWS Wave Hindcast Backbone:** The local Copernicus North West Shelf wave reanalysis files are treated as an independent `nws_` prefixed farm/sample-point hindcast source, not only as an AIS event join. Planning is based on turbine coordinates and commissioning dates, with AIS dates used only as supporting evidence. The primary wave output is partitioned under `Data/Processed/metocean/nws_wave_timeseries/`; event-level `nws_event_features.parquet` remains a secondary join product. NWS hourly true-current evidence is handled separately by the accepted NWS current archive and Current Confidence v1.
- **Baltic Copernicus Wave Archive:** The Baltic branch targets `BALTICSEA_MULTIYEAR_WAV_003_015` / `cmems_mod_bal_wav_my_PT1H-i` for Baltic wave coverage that the local NWS archive does not provide consistently. The project has reviewed subset downloads saved under `Data/Raw/Metocean/CMEMS/BalticSea/Waves/`, planned from commissioning/operation start to the configured study end date (`2024-12-31`) using turbine footprint plus buffer. Baltic coverage is currently source-limited by `Data/Interim/European_Turbine_Coordinates.csv`: only 17 farms in the project inventory fall inside the Baltic product domain, and 16 are in scope for the current study window because `Baltic Eagle` starts in 2025. AIS events remain validation samples and supporting date evidence only; they do not define the archive boundary. `src/om_pipeline/metocean/baltic_wave_materializer.py` has materialized the reviewed raw subsets into native-hourly, source-labelled `baltic_` wave partitions under `Data/Processed/metocean/baltic_wave_timeseries/`; the accepted archive contains `16` farms, `238` partitions, and `73,866,720` rows. `VSDX` and `VSDY` are Stokes drift components, not Eulerian current u/v; a separate Baltic ocean physics/current product is still required.
- **Static Bathymetry Site Context:** Bathymetry is a static source-specific site-context layer, not a metocean time-series or dwell-table rebuild. `src/om_pipeline/metocean/bathymetry_planner.py` and `scripts/plan_bathymetry_assignment.py` provide the planning dry-run from `analysis/06_rq6_metocean_spatial_resolution/common_metocean_farm_requirements.csv`, with EMODnet Bathymetry as the primary source and `GEBCO_2026` as a documented cross-check source. The accepted implementation path in ADR 0022 uses `src/om_pipeline/metocean/bathymetry_assignment.py` and `scripts/assign_bathymetry_to_metocean_points.py` to cache official EMODnet `depth_sample` REST responses under `Data/Raw/Metocean/Bathymetry/` and write `Data/Processed/metocean/bathymetry/site_bathymetry_points.parquet` with positive-down `water_depth_m`, source/version, vertical datum, assignment method, and spatial match status. The first full assignment covered `119` farms and `6,642` common metocean sample points with zero missing depths and zero duplicate farm/sample keys; `GEBCO_2026` was not fetched because EMODnet returned valid samples for all points.
- **FINO Validation Planning:** FINO1, FINO2, and FINO3 are treated as fixed-station validation and baseline evidence, not automatic farm-wide metocean sources. ADR 0023 records the dry-run-only access plan implemented by `src/om_pipeline/metocean/fino_metadata_planner.py` and `scripts/plan_fino_metadata_access.py`. The first report, `analysis/06_rq6_metocean_spatial_resolution/fino_metadata_access_plan.md`, records BSH-Login / Insitu access requirements, public station metadata, station-to-farm proximity based on the accepted bathymetry sample-point table, proposed metadata/time-series schemas, and validation gates. No FINO bulk download or time-series import has been run; portal variable names, measurement heights/depths, QC flags, file formats, and final licence wording still require a small post-access pilot.
- **FINO Native Export Inspection:** ADR 0024 records the report-only FINO export inspector implemented by `src/om_pipeline/metocean/fino_export_inspector.py` and `scripts/inspect_fino_export.py`. It is designed for one small manually exported BSH Insitu CSV/ASCII file, initially FINO1 wave data near Alpha Ventus. The inspector detects delimiter/header structure, timestamp parsing, inferred cadence, likely `Hs`, `Tp`, wave-direction, unit, and QC/status columns, then writes a Markdown inspection report. It does not import FINO time series, write processed FINO parquet, scrape the portal, handle credentials, source-fuse, interpolate, download currents, rerun NORA3, or rebuild dwell-metocean tables.
- **Generalized Upscaling & QA:** The metocean ingestor dynamically processes all metocean parameters to upscale hourly records to the 10-minute backbone: Cubic Spline interpolation is used for all scalar columns (e.g. `hs`, `tp`, `wind_speed_10m`, `wind_speed_100m`, `current_speed`) and Circular Vector Interpolation is used for all angular columns (e.g. `wave_direction`, `wind_direction_10m`, `wind_direction_100m`, `current_direction`). The QA gate enforces strict [0, 360) angular bounds and zero nulls.
- **SCADA Handshake & De-Anonymization:** 
    - **Taxonomy-based Labeling:** Cross-references vessel dwell events with turbine operational states in `src/om_pipeline/analysis/scada_handshake.py`. It maps turbine states (specifically status 3: Service) to clean O&M label matrices, categorizing them as `maintenance_success`, `standby_weather`, or `attempted_transfer` based on dwell duration and proximity thresholds.
    - **Wind Farm B De-Anonymization:** Identified as **Alpha Ventus** (REpower/Senvion 5M assets) with a **0-year temporal shift** (Shift = 0). File timestamps map to true operating calendar dates (February 2022 to February 2023).
    - **Wind Farm C De-Anonymization:** High-confidence production working mapping to **Trianel Windpark Borkum I & II** with a **0-year temporal shift** (Shift = 0). CAREtoCompare timestamps map directly to the working true operating calendar (2022-2024). The turbine fingerprint indicates a mixed Borkum I/Borkum II fleet, and the AIS/SCADA co-occurrence test remains a coverage-limited validation enhancement rather than a pipeline blocker.

### Empirical Multi-Parameter Workability Surfaces
- **Provisional Stage 1 Workability Surface:** `src/om_pipeline/analysis/workability.py`, `src/om_pipeline/analysis/provisional_stage1_hs_tp.py`, and `scripts/build_provisional_stage1_hs_tp_surface.py` build the explicitly labelled `Provisional NORA3-derived Tier A wave-only observed operational envelope` from `Data/Processed/ais_dwell_backfill/cross_farm_dwell_weather_features.parquet`. $H_s \times T_p$ is the default preset for Stage 1, not the closed definition of workability. The underlying surface spec supports configurable feature columns, bins, grouping columns, and long-form simulator-ready outputs so Stage 2 can add wind speed, current speed, vessel, task, and site dimensions without redefining the contract. This remains an observed successful-dwell envelope, not `P(operation | weather)`, and it does not infer CTV/SOV roles.
- **Metocean Fusion v0:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/metocean_fusion_v0.py` and `scripts/build_metocean_fusion_v0.py` build the first source-resolved wave plus bathymetry event table at `Data/Processed/metocean/fusion_v0/dwell_metocean_fusion_v0.parquet`. Fusion v0 preserves dwell row identity, prioritizes Baltic waves where covered, then NWS waves, then existing NORA3 active fields, and joins static EMODnet bathymetry by the nearest common sample point to each dwell centroid. It is a research increment for coverage and envelope sensitivity, not a final production dwell-metocean rebuild and not a calibrated access probability model.
- **Metocean Fusion v1 Source Agreement:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/metocean_fusion_v1_source_agreement.py` and `scripts/build_metocean_fusion_v1_source_agreement.py` turn the v0 coverage resolver into a wave-source agreement and confidence layer. Fusion v1 writes source candidate rows for NORA3, NWS, and Baltic, computes pairwise Hs/Tp/direction disagreement, and assigns event-level A/B/C/D wave confidence without treating weighted values as truth. It reads existing local accepted sources only, preserves missing-source reasons, and remains a research validation increment rather than a final source-fused dwell-metocean rebuild.
- **Current Pilot v1 True u/v Candidates:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/current_pilot_v1.py` and `scripts/run_current_pilot_v1.py` implement one-farm/year current pilots for true Eulerian `uo`/`vo` evidence using the same candidate/provenance/confidence pattern as Fusion v1. The pilot writes source-specific candidate rows under `Data/Processed/metocean/current_pilots/` and reports under `reports/current_pilot_v1/`, with event-scale suitability based on cadence, time gaps, bracketing, depth level, spatial distance, and provenance. Legacy `Data/Raw/Metocean/CMEMS/cmems_raw_*.csv` cache files and offline synthetic current outputs must not be promoted as research evidence because they lack `uo/vo`, depth, and dataset provenance.
- **NWS Current Scale Preflight:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/current_scaling_preflight.py` and `scripts/build_current_scaling_preflight.py` rank observed farm-years for a later NWS hourly current scale extraction without downloading currents. The preflight writes `Data/Processed/metocean/current_pilots/nws_current_scale_eligibility.parquet` and decision reports under `reports/current_pilot_v1/`. Normal scale requires NWS domain match, non-trivial dwell and Tier A counts, accepted bathymetry sample points, acceptable storage/runtime, and no dominance by `<=10 m` shallow points because the NWS product documentation warns against direct use in extensive shallow areas. Shallow/coastal or low-Tier-A but scientifically useful farm-years are labelled `stress_test_only`. The Baltic hourly-current decision remains metadata-only: historical Baltic physics reanalysis is true `uo/vo` but daily/contextual; recent Baltic analysis/forecast has hourly/sub-hourly true currents only for the later analysis/forecast period, not the 2010-2020 historical evidence window.
- **NWS Current Batch v1:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/nws_current_batch.py` and `scripts/run_nws_current_batch.py` implement the controlled NWS hourly true `uo`/`vo` current archive write from the accepted eligibility table. The first top-10 engineering batch validated the extraction path; the follow-on normal recommended scale run accepted `125` non-stress-test farm-years, `76,886,304` source rows, and zero failures under `Data/Processed/metocean/nws_current_timeseries/wind_farm=<farm>/year=<year>/part.parquet`. The manifest at `Data/Processed/metocean/nws_current_timeseries/manifest.csv` records `115` newly validated farm-years and `10` previously accepted partitions skipped/revalidated. Validation reports live under `reports/current_pilot_v1/nws_batch_top10/` and `reports/current_pilot_v1/nws_batch_remaining_recommended/`. Raw Copernicus subsets are cached only under the labelled pilot path `Data/Raw/Metocean/CMEMS/NorthWestShelf/Currents/Pilots/`, separate from legacy current CSV caches. The archive records flow-to current direction, source provenance, grid distance, depth-selection rule, EMODnet depth warnings, and NWS model bathymetry warnings; it remains a source-specific archive, not Fusion v2 or a dwell-metocean rebuild. Stress-test farm-years remain excluded pending separate shallow/coastal approval.
- **Current Confidence v1:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/current_confidence_v1.py` and `scripts/build_current_confidence_v1.py` attach the accepted NWS hourly true `uo`/`vo` archive to dwell events without downloading new data or rebuilding Fusion v2. The layer writes one event candidate row and one confidence row per dwell event under `Data/Processed/metocean/current_confidence_v1/`, preserving row identity, missing NWS partitions, event-window/bracketing sample counts, nearest time gaps, flow-to direction, speed consistency, EMODnet depth warnings, wave-confidence overlap, and A/B/C/D current confidence. The accepted v1 output has `92,660` candidate/confidence rows, `16,307` NWS event-scale assignments, and `5,358` Tier A valid-current assignments; non-covered farm-years remain explicit `D_unsuitable` rows rather than zero-current assumptions. This is the current evidence layer required before Fusion v2.
- **Wind Confidence v1:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/wind_confidence_v1.py` and `scripts/build_wind_confidence_v1.py` formalize existing NORA3 active-window wind evidence before Fusion v2. The layer writes one wind candidate and one wind confidence row per dwell event under `Data/Processed/metocean/wind_confidence_v1/`, using only existing dwell-weather NORA3 wind fields plus read-only NORA3 raw/joined-cache inventory diagnostics. The accepted output has `92,660` candidate/confidence rows, `75,380` speed-ready events, `13,708` Tier A speed-ready events, and only `197` speed+direction events. Most usable rows are `B_speed_only`; wind direction remains sparse and repair-pending because the local raw NORA3 wind cache is overwhelmingly speed-only (`33,521` speed-only files versus `464` speed+direction files). Missing wind direction must remain null and must not be interpreted as zero direction. Fusion v2 should include wind speed and wind confidence, with wind direction nullable or sensitivity-only until a targeted repair is approved.
- **Metocean Fusion v2:** `src/om_pipeline/metocean/metocean_fusion_v2.py` and `scripts/build_metocean_fusion_v2.py` build the first combined source-resolved event feature table at `Data/Processed/metocean/fusion_v2/dwell_metocean_fusion_v2.parquet`. Fusion v2 preserves all `92,660` dwell rows and joins Wave Confidence v1, Wind Confidence v1, Current Confidence v1, and EMODnet bathymetry by stable dwell identity plus nearest accepted site-context sample point. It keeps wave, wind, current, and bathymetry confidence/provenance separate, then adds modelling-readiness flags for wave-only, wave+wind, wave+current, wave+wind+current, and high-confidence multivariate subsets. Wind speed is modelling-ready for `75,380` events, but wind direction remains nullable and sensitivity-only (`197` events). Current values are used only for `A_event_scale` NWS assignments (`16,307` events); missing or non-covered current remains null and is never treated as zero. Fusion v2 is an accepted/provisional event feature layer for the next Stage 2 sensitivity branch, not a final model and not calibrated access probability. The completed evidence-readiness audit records recommendation `proceed_with_restrictions` with caveats `partial_event_scale_current_coverage`, `wind_direction_sensitivity_only`, and `depth_warning_sensitivity_required`.
- **Non-Linear Tp Wave Period Interaction:** The research hypothesis is that observed operating envelopes depend on the joint $H_s \times T_p$ sea state rather than a static wave-height threshold. Provisional Stage 1 results may describe observed upper envelopes in wave-period bins, but they must not be interpreted as validated operational limits without failed attempts, non-operation windows, SCADA/WoW labels, or another denominator.
- **95th Percentile Boundary Envelope:** The observed successful-dwell boundary per wave period bin is estimated at the 95th percentile significant wave height ($H_s$) of successful Tier A (Asset-Proximal) dwells, treating high-end points as an empirical upper envelope rather than a certified access limit.
- **Provisional Lookup / Envelope Matrix:** The Stage 1 matrix is an empirical observed-envelope or relative-workability heuristic derived from successful Tier A dwell events. It is not a calibrated access probability $P(\text{Access} | H_s, T_p)$ until non-operation weather windows, failed attempts, or SCADA/WoW labels are added.
- **Exploratory Vessel-Size Sensitivity:** Provisional analysis may stratify observed envelopes by `vessel_length_m` bands as exploratory sensitivity only. These bands must not be labelled CTV/SOV unless validated by an external registry or authoritative vessel taxonomy.

## Ingestion Logic & Safety
- **Robust Headers:** Uses a synonym-based resolver (`Latitude`, `Longitude`, `SOG`) to handle multi-year DMA schema variations.
- **Numeric Normalization:** Handles comma decimal delimiters common in European datasets.
- **Counter-based Validation:** Each ingestion cycle tracks detailed metrics (scanned, kept, malformed skips) for provenance.
- **Data Deduplication & Validation Gate:** Interim event files (`OM_Events_*.csv`) are audited and purged in-place based on the logical composite key `[Timestamp, vessel_id, event_type]` mapping to physical columns `[start, MMSI, event_class]` using a memory-efficient DuckDB partitioning algorithm. Future duplicate creations on overlapping boundaries are prevented at runtime via in-memory deduplication during events generation. A hard validation gate is enforced at the start of Metocean Weather Ingestion, blocking downstream processing if any dirty interim files are detected.
- **Provenance:** Detailed pilot run metadata is preserved in `docs/provenance.md`.

## Technical Constraints
- **Storage:** Data is stored locally on high-speed SSDs. Large archives are streamed to minimize disk footprint.
- **Privacy:** Vessel names and MMSIs are handled for research but should not be exposed in public-facing summaries if they contain sensitive operational metadata.
